Squat Analyzer was my Master Thesis. The goal of this thesis was to create an application that uses 3D human pose estimation to track the correct form of a squat and provide real-time feedback on mistakes.

After researching 3D human pose estimation and the squat exercise, the most common mistakes were identified: wrong knee movement, bad back posture, too early hip movement, inefficient movement path, and insufficient depth. Based on that findings several pose estimation systems were evaluated with a marker based motion tracking system. The rgb camera based system MeTRABs and the depth camera based Azure Kinect camera were found to be suitable candidates.

An application was developed to track squat form based on these identified mistakes. This application was made with python and it is not depended on a specific pose estimation system. That means it can be exchanged which means Metrabs as well as Azure Kinect can be used. The application was tested on both system by 30 participants who did 10 squats each.

The results of the mistake detection were compared to an expert assessment and it was found to be effective in detecting the left knee path, depth, movement path, and shooting up of the hips.
