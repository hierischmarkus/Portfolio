MAHÜ 10-18 is a construction project of social significance in the heart of Vienna. My company Responsive Spaces created an interactive, digitally staged model that enables the building owner to communicate the architectural concept, construction site information as well as expected offers by means of two video projectors and displays. The moving model of the building becomes a canvas and grows with the content shown.

This model can be kinetically staged by means of a lifting column and thus serves as an exciting stage for the interactive communication of all content related to the construction project. Different heights of the building showcase content that includes the building's history, future use of the floors, and visual representations of their intended appearance.

I was involved in the early prototpying process. My primary focus was on developing an efficient mapping solution for the two projectors, ensuring that the projection content aligned seamlessly with the model. By creating a adequate projection matrix, I successfully synchronized the positions of both projectors and the model, guaranteeing accurate and visually appealing projections. Furthermore, I took charge of implementing some of the content designs.
